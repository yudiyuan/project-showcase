About Me

I chose software development because I believe developing software is a meaningful and impactful endeavor. Previously, I worked as an administrative assistant for two years, where my monthly tasks were almost identical, and the workflows rarely changed. Every time I mechanically completed the same tedious tasks, I wished I knew how to implement office automation to save a significant amount of time.

That’s why I am most interested in office automation—I want to help more office workers reduce their workload. I also find it fascinating to develop intelligent task management programs. For instance, if new assignments are posted on the school’s website, Brightspace, the system could automatically organize them based on course and due date, displaying all tasks in one place. This would be incredibly helpful for students like me, who often worry about missing deadlines or being unaware of assignments.

One of my greatest strengths is my habit of scheduling tasks and tracking the time spent on each one. I regularly summarize my progress on a weekly basis and analyze long-term tasks, allowing me to clearly see my improvements. I believe this is a valuable habit in the workplace as well, helping me stay organized and in control of all tasks.

I aspire to become a Java developer, whether in a specialized tech company or a traditional industry. Personally, I prefer experiencing different business sectors, as this broadens my knowledge and understanding of the world. It also enhances my flexibility in applying coding skills across various domains.
